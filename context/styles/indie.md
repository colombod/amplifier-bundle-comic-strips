# Indie Style Pack

Visual style guide for indie comic book strip creation.

## Image Prompt Template

Base template for generating indie-style panel images:

```
Indie comic book illustration, gritty painterly style, dark atmospheric, {scene_description}. Complex shading with textured brush strokes. Moody lighting with deep shadows. Mature artistic tone in the style of Top Cow and Image Comics. Detailed backgrounds with environmental storytelling. No text in image.
```

## Color Palette

Muted, desaturated tones with selective vivid accents for dramatic emphasis.

- **Primary**: Muted desaturated core tones for grounding and atmosphere:
  - Dark teal `#2D4A4A` — environments, shadows, moody undertones
  - Burnt sienna `#8B4513` — skin tones, wood, earth, warmth
  - Slate `#4A5568` — concrete, metal, clothing, neutral surfaces
- **Secondary**: Depth and darkness:
  - Deep blacks `#0D0D0D` — heavy shadows, night scenes, silhouettes
  - Warm grays `#78716C` — smoke, fog, weathered surfaces, midtones
- **Highlights**: Selective vivid accents used sparingly for maximum impact:
  - Blood red `#8B0000` — violence, danger, emotional intensity
  - Electric blue `#00B4D8` — energy, technology, supernatural elements
- **Backgrounds**: Textured dark with haze — layered atmospheric washes suggesting depth and mood

## Panel Conventions

- **Reading direction**: LEFT-TO-RIGHT — panels are arranged and read from left to right, top to bottom, following standard Western comic flow
- **Borders**: Irregular borderless and bleeding borders; panels merge into each other or bleed off the page edge for immersive effect
- **Gutters**: Variable 6-16px gutters, sometimes black to reinforce dark mood and separate fragmented compositions
- **Border shapes**: Irregular overlapping fragmented shapes; panels layer and intersect for visual tension and narrative complexity
- **Splash panels**: Atmospheric establishing shots — wide cinematic panels that set mood and environment before action
- **Panel count**: 3-6 per page in an asymmetric layout; variable sizing creates visual rhythm and pacing control

## Text Treatment

- **Speech bubbles**: Irregular hand-drawn bubbles with rough edges; organic imperfect shapes that feel sketched and authentic
- **Font style**: Handwritten serif/script mixed case lettering — lowercase-dominant with natural variation in size and slant
- **Sound effects**: Integrated textured weathered sound effects that blend into the artwork as distressed graphic elements rather than floating text
- **Caption boxes**: Dark boxes with light text for narration — somber tonal captions that feel like internal monologue
- **Dialogue philosophy**: Minimal dialogue with emphasis on visual storytelling — let the art carry the narrative weight

## Character Rendering

- **Proportions**: Realistic proportions with stylized features — grounded anatomy with artistic interpretation of form
- **Shading**: Heavy shadow with dramatic contrast; deep blacks define volume and mood across faces and figures
- **Texture**: Textured brush strokes visible in rendering — painterly quality with visible hand of the artist
- **Expressions**: Subtle expressions conveyed through eyes and body language rather than exaggerated features
- **Environment**: Environmental interaction — characters exist within and respond to their surroundings, not posed against flat backdrops
- **Philosophy**: Complex, not idealized — flawed, human, and believable characters with visual depth and narrative weight

## AmpliVerse Branding

- **Watermark**: Subtle watermark placed in the gutter space between panels, barely visible to maintain immersion
- **Avatar**: AmpliVerse avatar positioned at the bottom center, small and understated to respect the artistic tone
- **Color treatment**: Muted coloring matched to the issue's palette — branding should feel integrated, never intrusive
