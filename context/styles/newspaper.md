# Newspaper Style Pack

Visual style guide for newspaper comic strip creation.

## Image Prompt Template

Base template for generating newspaper-style panel images:

```
Newspaper comic strip illustration, clean simple line art, minimal detail, {scene_description}. Flat colors with no gradients. Simple backgrounds or solid color fields. Charming character designs with round features. In the style of Peanuts and Calvin and Hobbes. No text in image.
```

## Color Palette

Clean, minimal palette with flat pastels on a black-and-white foundation.

- **Primary**: Clean black `#000000` on white `#FFFFFF` — ink lines and paper background forming the core contrast
- **Fill**: Flat pastels for simple, cheerful coloring:
  - Sky blue `#87CEEB` — skies, water, calm backgrounds
  - Grass green `#90EE90` — lawns, trees, nature elements
  - Warm yellow `#FFFACD` — sunlight, sand, warm accents
- **Accents**: Simple red `#FF6B6B` — scarves, hearts, highlights, and comedic emphasis

## Panel Conventions

- **Reading direction**: LEFT-TO-RIGHT — panels are arranged and read from left to right in a single horizontal row, following classic newspaper strip flow
- **Borders**: Thin uniform 1px black borders around every panel — clean and consistent
- **Gutters**: Consistent narrow 6px white gutters between panels — tight spacing keeps the strip compact
- **Border shapes**: Equal-width rectangles in a single horizontal row — no variation in panel size or shape
- **Panel count**: 3-4 panel strip in classic newspaper format — the standard daily strip length
- **Layout**: Strictly regular grid — no overlapping, no bleeds, no asymmetry

## Text Treatment

- **Speech bubbles**: Simple round white bubbles with thin black outline — clean and unobtrusive
- **Font style**: Clean sans-serif sentence case lettering — friendly and readable at small sizes
- **Sound effects**: Rare sound effects used for comedic emphasis only — a well-placed "BONK" or "CRASH" for punchline impact
- **Caption boxes**: Minimal captions — used sparingly for time/place context only, never for narration
- **Dialogue philosophy**: Punchline in final panel following setup-setup-payoff structure — every strip builds to a comedic beat

## Character Rendering

- **Proportions**: Simple round heads with dot eyes — expressive faces from minimal marks
- **Detail level**: Minimal detail with emphasis on silhouette recognition — each character identifiable by shape alone
- **Scale**: Consistent 60% panel height for standing characters — uniform sizing across all panels
- **Coloring**: Flat coloring with no shading — clean fills without gradients, shadows, or highlights
- **Expressions**: Exaggerated body language to convey emotion — arms thrown wide, slumped shoulders, dramatic postures
- **Philosophy**: Cute simplified agents — charming, approachable character designs that prioritize personality over realism

## AmpliVerse Branding

- **Masthead**: Masthead banner across top of the strip — clean title area spanning the full strip width
- **Avatar**: AmpliVerse avatar positioned left of title in the masthead — small and integrated into the strip header
- **Color treatment**: Clean black on white for all branding elements — matching the strip's minimal aesthetic
